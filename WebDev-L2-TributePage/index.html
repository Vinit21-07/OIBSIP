The Manifesto

01

Dream

A boatman's son from a temple town believed that dreams were not what you see in sleep, but what refuses to let you sleep. He carried that belief from the shores of Rameswaram to the launch pads of a rising republic.

02

Ignite

He built rockets and missiles, but insisted that the real ignition happened in young minds. Every lecture was a launch sequence; every student, a payload of possibility for the country he loved.

03

Serve

Power never softened him. As President he stayed a teacher — plain-living, endlessly curious, forever asking the young of India to aim higher than the sky he had already reached.

A Life in Full

Biography · 1931 — 2015

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, a small island town in Tamil Nadu. His father ferried pilgrims across the sea and his family lived simply; as a boy Kalam sold newspapers to help at home. Watching seabirds ride the wind, he grew fascinated by the mystery of flight — a curiosity that would shape the rest of his life.

He studied physics and then aeronautical engineering in Chennai, and joined India's defence research establishment before moving to the space programme. As project director he led the effort behind the SLV-III, and in 1980 the vehicle placed the Rohini satellite in orbit — a defining moment that announced India's arrival as a space-faring nation.

Through the following decades Kalam guided the development of indigenous missiles such as Agni and Prithvi, earning the affectionate title of the "Missile Man of India." He also played a central organisational role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests of 1998. For his contributions to science and to the nation, he received the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honour.

In 2002 he became the eleventh President of India, and his warmth, humility and love for the young earned him the name the "People's President." Even after office he never stopped teaching. On 27 July 2015 he collapsed while doing what he loved most — lecturing to students at IIM Shillong — and passed away, leaving behind a nation of dreamers he had personally set alight.
